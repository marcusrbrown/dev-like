## Sourcing Constraints
All `dev-like` profiles are synthesized strictly from **public sources only**. We use official APIs, published repositories, technical blogs, and conference talks. We extract principles and workflow shapes rather than reproducing prose.

## Provenance
Every generated profile is **versioned at generation**. Receipts are hard-linked for every claim: no source, no claim.

## Consent Tiers
Every profile exists under one of four consent tiers, listed in descending order of strength:
- `self-published`: The subject ships its own culture artifacts and workflow rules.
- `stated`: The target has explicitly stated or consented to their workflows being used in this manner.
- `observed`: Workflows observed from public repositories or technical outputs.
- `social`: Insights gleaned from public social and technical communications.

## Privacy Floor
The **`stated`** tier acts as a strict floor for people. We do not generate profiles for individual developers unless they meet or exceed this consent threshold.

## Profile Requests & Control
You can request new organization or framework profiles via the <a href="https://github.com/marcusrbrown/dev-like/issues/new?template=profile-request.yml" data-umami-event="request-profile">profile request issue template</a>.

If you or your organization has been profiled and you wish to have it removed, please use the <a href="https://github.com/marcusrbrown/dev-like/issues/new?template=optout.yml" data-umami-event="opt-out">opt-out path</a>. We commit to a **48-hour** response promise to honor your opt-out. Read the full <a href="https://github.com/marcusrbrown/dev-like/blob/main/registry/OPTOUT.md" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">opt-out policy source</a> for more details.
